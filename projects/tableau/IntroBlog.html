Over the past 50 years, diets around the world have changed

Diets are more diverse

Diversity reflects the number of unique crops contributing to a diet, and the dominance of each crop’s contribution to the total food supply.

Diets across the globe are comprised of more unique crop products and are less dominated by one particular crop. Globally, the number of crops that comprise our average diet has increased 30% from 27 crops to 36 crops between 1961-2009. On average, the most dominant foods in a diet have become less important in contributing calories.

The Bangladesh diet is the least diverse diet comprised of 27 crops, with 70% of the calories coming from rice alone. Conversely, Cameroon has the most diverse diet comprised of 36 unique crops each contributing more equally to the diet.
